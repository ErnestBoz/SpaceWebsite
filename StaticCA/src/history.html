First Human in Space

On April 12, 1961, Yuri Gagarin became the first human to journey into outer space aboard Vostok 1.

Moon Landing

On July 20, 1969, Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin became the first humans to walk on the Moon during the Apollo 11 mission.

International Space Station

Launched in 1998, the ISS serves as a space environment research laboratory, fostering international collaboration.

Recent Mars Exploration

In recent years, missions like Perseverance and Curiosity have explored the Martian surface, searching for signs of past life.
















Key Achievements in Space Exploration

Sputnik 1 - first satellite in space (1957)

Laika - first animal to orbit the Earth (1957)

Yuri Gagarin - first human in space (1961)

Apollo 11 - first moon landing (1969)

Salyut 1 - first space station (1971)

Voyager 1 - first interplanetary exploration (1977)

Hubble telescope (1990)

Sojourner - first Mars rover (1997)

International space station (1998)

Rosetta/Philae - first comet landing (2014)

New Horizons - first Pluto flyby(2015)

James Webb telescope (2021)
