Europa Clipper

Europa Clipper, a NASA mission launched in 2024, will arrive at Jupiter’s moon Europa by 2030. The spacecraft will perform dozens of flybys, searching for signs of a subsurface ocean and conditions that could support life.

Artemis 3

Launching in 2027, Artemis III, led by NASA, will mark humanity’s return to the Moon by landing near the lunar south pole. The mission aims to establish a sustainable presence and conduct vital scientific research for future deep-space exploration.

Mars Sample Return

The Mars Sample Return mission, a collaboration between NASA and ESA, is planned for launch in 2026. It will retrieve rock and soil samples collected by the Perseverance rover, which will be studied on Earth to uncover Mars’ geological history and potential for past life.

Lunar Gateway

The Lunar Gateway, a joint project by NASA, ESA, JAXA, and CSA, is expected to begin assembly in 2028. This space station will orbit the Moon, supporting Artemis missions, providing a staging ground for deep-space travel, and enabling long-term lunar exploration.

Dragonfly

Planned for the early 2030s, Dragonfly, a NASA mission, will send a nuclear-powered drone to Saturn’s largest moon, Titan. This rotorcraft will explore the surface and atmosphere, searching for organic compounds and clues about life’s origins.
